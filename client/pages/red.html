All Things Common in Ancient Times

In ancient times groups of righteous saints practiced the strategy of “all things common”.

In the new testament on the day of Pentecost the Holy Spirit was poured out and Peter testifies of Jesus’s resurrection. The people’s hearts were pricked by the Holy Ghost and they asked Peter what they should do. Peter responded that they should repent and be baptized, and that day 3000 souls were baptized. Afterwards it says, “And all that believed were together, and had all things common; And sold their possessions and goods, and parted them to all men, as every man had need (Acts 2:44:45). I do not know whether having all things common among them was a policy of the church at this time, or if they simply did it out of the goodness of their heart. The point is that they were able to rise above personal selfishness and give of what they had to help those around them who had less.

In the Book of Mormon after the visitation of Jesus Christ the people lived very happily. The wicked had been destroyed in the natural disasters and destruction that followed Christ’s death on the cross, and the righteous had spent a good period of time being taught by the Savior Himself. In Fourth Nephi it records that, “the people were all converted unto the Lord” (4 Nephi 1:2). All were striving to live the gospel as they had just been instructed, and as part of that “they had all things common among them; therefore there were not rich and poor, bond and free” (4 Nephi 1:3). Later Mormon says, “…and surely there could not be a happier people among all the people who had been created by the hand of God.” (4 Nephi 1:16). I am sure that their love for their neighbors and their selflessness played a large roll in creating such a happy people.
